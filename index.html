About Himself

Lionel Messi, often hailed as one of the greatest footballers of all time, has left an indelible mark on the sport through his unparalleled talent, extraordinary skill, and remarkable consistency. Born on June 24, 1987, in Rosario, Argentina, Messi's journey to football stardom began at a young age when he joined the youth team of his local club, Newell's Old Boys. At the age of 13, Messi moved to Spain to join FC Barcelona's famed La Masia youth academy, where his exceptional abilities quickly became apparent. Making his first-team debut for Barcelona at just 17 years old in 2004, Messi soon established himself as a key player for the club, mesmerizing fans and opponents alike with his agility, vision, and goal-scoring prowess. Throughout his illustrious career at Barcelona, Messi amassed a staggering number of records and accolades. He became the club's all-time leading goal scorer, surpassing the legendary figures of the sport. Messi's unparalleled dribbling skills, precise passing, and instinctive ability to find the back of the net earned him numerous individual awards, including a record six Ballon d'Or titles, awarded to the world's best player. Notably, Messi's partnership with fellow Barcelona stars such as Xavi Hernández and Andrés Iniesta formed the backbone of one of the most dominant eras in football history. Under the guidance of iconic coaches like Pep Guardiola and Luis Enrique, Barcelona won numerous domestic and international titles, including multiple UEFA Champions League trophies. Despite his immense success at Barcelona, Messi's career took an unexpected turn in 2021 when he left the club due to financial constraints and contractual disputes. He then joined Paris Saint-Germain (PSG), where he continued to showcase his extraordinary talent on the pitch. In addition to his club success, Messi has also enjoyed considerable achievements with the Argentine national team. Despite facing criticism for his performances at the international level early in his career, Messi led Argentina to victory in the 2021 Copa America, securing his first major international trophy and cementing his legacy as one of the greatest players in football history. Beyond his on-field exploits, Messi is admired for his humility, professionalism, and philanthropy. He has used his platform to support numerous charitable causes, including children's healthcare and education initiatives. In summary, Lionel Messi's impact on football transcends statistics and trophies. His unique blend of skill, creativity, and determination has captivated audiences around the world, inspiring generations of footballers and earning him a place among the sport's all-time greats.

Career

Despite his dual citizenship and professional success in Spain, Messi’s ties with his homeland remained strong, and he was a key member of various Argentine national teams from 2005. He played on Argentina’s victorious 2005 FIFA World Youth Championship squad, represented the country in the 2006 World Cup, and scored two goals in five matches as Argentina swept to the gold medal at the Beijing 2008 Olympic Games. Messi helped Argentina reach the 2010 World Cup quarterfinals, where the team was eliminated by Germany for the second consecutive time in World Cup play. At the 2014 World Cup, Messi put on a dazzling display, scoring four goals and almost single-handedly propelling an offense-deficient Argentina team through the group stage and into the knockout rounds, where Argentina then advanced to the World Cup final for the first time in 24 years. Argentina lost that contest 1–0 to Germany, but Messi nevertheless won the Golden Ball award as the tournament’s best player. During the 2016 Copa América Centenario tournament, he netted his 55th international goal to break Gabriel Batistuta’s Argentine scoring record. After Argentina was defeated in the Copa final—the team’s third consecutive finals loss in a major tournament—Messi said that he was quitting the national team, but his short-lived “retirement” lasted less than two months before he announced his return to the Argentine team. At the 2018 World Cup, he helped an overmatched Argentine side reach the knockout stage, where they were eliminated by eventual champion France in their first match. After a third-place finish at the 2019 Copa América, Messi led Argentina to victory in the tournament two years later, and he received the Golden Ball award. His success continued at the 2022 World Cup. There he guided Argentina to the finals, where he scored two goals—and made a penalty kick during the shootout—to help defeat France. Messi won the World Cup’s Golden Ball, becoming the first male player to receive that award twice. In addition, his outstanding play in the tournament was instrumental in Messi winning his eighth Ballon d’Or in 2023.

Messi's World Cup legacy

Messi scored seven goals at the 2022 World Cup, just one fewer than eventual Golden Boot winner Kylian Mbappe. He finished the tournament with the joint-highest number of assists (three) and key passes (21), and recorded more shots on target than any other player (13). The former PSG and Barcelona forward also became the first player in World Cup history to score in the group stage, last 16, quarter-final, semi-final and final at a single tournament - while picking up a record haul of five Man of the Match awards. When the time came to hand out the Golden Ball for the best player of the Qatar competition, there could only be one winner. Messi is now the only man to ever claim the prize twice, having also been recognised for his performances at the 2014 World Cup in Brazil. Messi has played at five World Cups in total, and is unmatched when it comes to overall goal contributions, with 13 goals and eight assists to his name. He also boasts the most appearances at the tournament on 26, one more than Germany icon Lothar Matthaus. Only three players have scored more World Cup goals than Messi - Miroslav Klose (16), Ronaldo Nazario (15), and Gerd Muller (14). For so long, Diego Maradona was the unrivalled hero to supporters across Argentina for masterminding their 1986 World Cup success. But Messi is no longer in his shadow, and the statistics prove that he is right up there with the greatest performers to have ever graced international football's most prestigious tournament.

Achievements

Lionel Messi's list of achievements in football is nothing short of extraordinary, reflecting his unparalleled talent, dedication, and consistency throughout his illustrious career. Here are some of the key achievements of Lionel Messi:
1. **Ballon d'Or**: Messi has won the prestigious Ballon d'Or award a record eight times (as of 2024), highlighting his dominance as the world's best player.
2. **Club Titles**: Messi has won numerous domestic and international titles with FC Barcelona and Paris Saint-Germain, including La Liga, Copa del Rey, UEFA Champions League, FIFA Club World Cup, and more.
3. **Goalscoring Records**: He holds numerous goalscoring records, including being Barcelona's all-time leading scorer, the player with the most goals in a calendar year (91 goals in 2012), and the player with the most hat-tricks in La Liga history.
4. **Assist Records**: Messi has also set records for the most assists in La Liga history and holds the record for the most assists in a single La Liga season.
5. **Individual Awards**: In addition to the Ballon d'Or, Messi has won numerous other individual awards, including FIFA World Player of the Year, UEFA Best Player in Europe, and FIFA World Cup Golden Ball.
6. **Olympic Gold Medal**: Messi won a gold medal with the Argentine national team at the 2008 Beijing Olympics.
7. **Copa America**: After years of near-misses, Messi finally won the Copa America with Argentina in 2021, marking his first major international trophy with the senior national team.
8. **Goal-Scoring Milestones**: Messi has scored over 700 career goals for club and country, making him one of the highest-scoring players in football history.
9. **Records for Argentina**: Messi holds various records for the Argentine national team, including being the all-time leading scorer and the player with the most assists.
10. **Impact on the Game**: Messi's influence extends beyond individual awards and records. He has inspired countless players around the world and has left an indelible mark on the sport of football through his style of play and unmatched skill.
These achievements only scratch the surface of Lionel Messi's incredible career. His legacy as one of the greatest footballers of all time is firmly cemented in the annals of the sport's history.

Copa America 2021: The First international Trophy

Lionel Messi's victory in the Copa America 2021 was a monumental moment in his illustrious career and for Argentine football as a whole. After years of near-misses and heartbreaks in international competitions, Messi finally lifted a major trophy with the Argentine national team. Throughout the tournament, Messi played a pivotal role in guiding Argentina to success. He led by example on the pitch, showcasing his exceptional skill, vision, and leadership qualities. Messi's performances were instrumental in Argentina's journey to the final, where they faced arch-rivals Brazil. In the final match against Brazil, held at the Maracanã Stadium in Rio de Janeiro, Messi delivered a masterclass performance, orchestrating Argentina's attacks and creating numerous goal-scoring opportunities. His determination and brilliance were evident throughout the game as he inspired his team to a hard-fought 1-0 victory over the host nation. The decisive moment of the final came in the 22nd minute when Angel Di Maria scored the only goal of the match, capitalizing on a sublime pass from Messi to beat the Brazilian goalkeeper. It was a moment of sheer joy and relief for Messi, his teammates, and millions of Argentine fans worldwide. With the victory in the Copa America 2021, Messi fulfilled a long-held dream of winning a major international trophy with Argentina. The triumph was particularly significant for Messi, who had faced criticism and scrutiny over his performances with the national team, despite his remarkable success at the club level. The Copa America victory solidified Messi's status as one of the greatest footballers of all time and cemented his legacy as a legend of Argentine football. It was a moment of redemption and glory for Messi, who had endured heartbreaks in previous international tournaments. For Argentine fans, the victory in the Copa America 2021 was a cause for celebration and jubilation, ending a 28-year wait for a major international trophy. Messi's heroics and leadership will be remembered as an integral part of Argentina's historic triumph in the Copa America 2021.
